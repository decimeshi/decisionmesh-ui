# DecisionMesh — CXO Briefing Script

**Audience:** CEOs, CFOs, and other non-technical / lightly-technical decision makers
**Goal:** Show, in business terms, why DecisionMesh keeps their AI usage safe, compliant, affordable, and under control.
**Length:** ~6–8 minutes spoken. Each section is one idea + one concrete example.

> Notes for the presenter are in *italics*. Anything in **[BRACKETS]** should be swapped for a real DecisionMesh figure or customer detail before you present.

---

## Opening (30 seconds)

"Every company is racing to put AI into its business. The hard part isn't turning it on — it's staying in control once it's on. Who's allowed to use it? What data is it touching? What is it costing us this month? And if a model starts misbehaving, how fast can we shut it down?

DecisionMesh is the control layer that answers all of those questions in one place. Let me walk you through four things it does for you, and then show you the single view your CEO and CFO will actually look at."

---

## 1. Compliance — *government regulation & internal audit*

**The message:**
"AI decisions can't be a black box when a regulator or your own audit team comes knocking. DecisionMesh records what every model did, on whose behalf, and with what data — so 'prove it' becomes a two-minute export instead of a two-week fire drill."

**Example:**
"Say your **[internal audit / compliance]** team needs to show that no customer financial data was fed into an external AI model last quarter. Without a control layer, that's people digging through logs for days. With DecisionMesh, you filter to that data type, pull the full trail of every request that touched it, and hand the auditor a signed report — same afternoon. You move from *hoping* you're compliant to *demonstrating* it on demand."

*Tie-back: 'That's the difference between a passing audit and a finding.'*

---

## 2. Security — *guardrails around who and what*

**The message:**
"Most AI risk isn't a dramatic hack — it's ordinary use going somewhere it shouldn't. Sensitive data leaving the building, or the wrong person getting access. DecisionMesh sets the rules once, and enforces them on every single request automatically."

**Example:**
"An employee pastes a document containing customer records into an AI tool to 'quickly summarize it.' Innocent intent — real exposure. DecisionMesh catches that sensitive data at the point of use and blocks or masks it before it ever leaves your environment, and flags it for your security team. One quiet policy prevents a breach you'd otherwise be explaining to your customers and regulators."

*Tie-back: 'The best security incident is the one that never becomes an incident.'*

---

## 3. Cost — *budgets and total spend*

**The message:**
"AI spend has a habit of arriving as a surprise. Usage is invisible until the bill lands. DecisionMesh makes every dollar visible in real time — by team, by project, by model — and lets you set hard budget limits before you overspend, not after."

**Example:**
"One team's experiment quietly scales up and starts burning **[$X,000]** a week. On a normal invoice you'd find out 30 days later. With DecisionMesh, you'd have seen the spike the same day, seen exactly which team and use case caused it, and — if you'd set a cap — it would have throttled automatically at budget. You stop paying to *discover* overspend and start *preventing* it."

*Tie-back: 'This is the difference between an AI budget and an AI hope.'*

---

## 4. Kill Switch — *shut down a rogue model instantly*

**The message:**
"Sometimes a model starts producing bad, biased, or unsafe output — or an integration goes wrong. When that happens, minutes matter. DecisionMesh gives you one button to instantly stop a specific model, tool, or integration across the whole company, without taking everything else down."

**Example:**
"Imagine a new model version starts giving customers confidently wrong answers. Instead of scrambling engineers to figure out how to disconnect it, an authorized leader hits the kill switch. That model is off everywhere in seconds, the rest of your AI keeps running, and you've contained the damage while you investigate. It's the fire alarm and the sprinkler system in one."

*Tie-back: 'You hope you never use it. You'll be very glad it's there the day you do.'*

---

## 5. The CEO / CFO Snapshot — *everything above, on one screen*

**The message:**
"You don't have time to read four dashboards. So all of this — compliance status, security flags, spend against budget, and system health — rolls up into a single executive snapshot. One glance tells you whether your AI is safe, compliant, and on budget today."

**Example:**
"On a single screen the CFO sees: 'We're at **[68%]** of this month's AI budget, trending on target.' The CEO sees: 'Zero open security flags, all compliance controls green, all systems healthy.' No meeting required, no translation from a technical team. If something needs attention, it's the one item that isn't green — and you can act on it right there."

*Tie-back: 'This is the view that turns AI from something you worry about into something you manage.'*

---

## Close (30 seconds)

"So — DecisionMesh isn't another AI tool to adopt. It's the layer that lets you adopt AI *confidently*: compliant by default, secure by default, spend that's visible and capped, a kill switch when you need it, and one executive view over all of it.

The question isn't whether you'll use AI across the business — you already are. The question is whether you can prove it's under control. DecisionMesh is how you answer 'yes.'
